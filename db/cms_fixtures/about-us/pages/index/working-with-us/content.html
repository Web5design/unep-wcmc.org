Working at WCMC

Our dedicated, professional team includes about 100 scientific and support staff, as well as fellows and interns. See what positions are available at our vacancies page.

We provide a stimulating and supportive work environment and 93% of our team agree that we provide exposure to interesting people and new ideas.

Our five organisational values – Partnership, Integrity, Commitment, Expertise and Leadership – are fundamental in developing and encouraging our success. This booklet explains what our values mean to us.

We are situated close to the centre of Cambridge, a beautiful and exciting place to visit. To find out more about Cambridge, click here.

What we offer

We will help you develop your technical, strategic and project management skills, while working as part of a motivated team. We offer training and development opportunities, both on-the-job and through more formal courses. The normal working week is 37 hours, with 25 days annual holiday and 8 days of statutory bank holidays, but we can accommodate flexible work patterns.

We offer a competitive salary and benefits package, including non-contributory life insurance, personal health insurance and a non-contributory pension after six months’ service. We also run a Childcare Voucher Scheme to assist working parents.

Academic and volunteering opportunities

Fellowships

We appoint exceptional individual scientists as honorary Fellows or Senior Fellows of UNEP-WCMC. This helps us access skills and advice of senior scientists, while providing our Fellows with unique professional opportunities.

Placements for MSc and MPhil Student Projects

EWe offer opportunities for students who are enrolled on a relevant university MSc or MPhil course to undertake their project work at the Centre. If you are interested in a student placement please contact recruitment@unep-wcmc.org.

Scholarships for the Cambridge MPhil degree in Conservation Leadership

Since 2010, the University of Cambridge has been working with UNEP-WCMC to deliver an MPhil degree in Conservation Leadership. Details of the course and scholarship opportunities can be found here.

Volunteering opportunities

We regularly offer unpaid internships to those keen to strengthen their professional experience in the biodiversity sector. Please contact recruitment@unep-wcmc.org and include a CV and covering letter outlining your interest and availability.
