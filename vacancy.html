Lead Economist

Wildlife and trade|Permanent position

£40,000 - £60,000

We are looking for a highly motivated and technically experienced Programme/Information Officer to work in the Protected Areas Programme and support the development, management and integration of WCMC’s suite of information systems, including inter alia the World Database of Protected Areas, the Protected Planet initiative, the registry of Indigenous and Community Conserved Areas, management effectiveness and social assessment datasets.

We are looking for a professional with the following experience:

  • Proven and relevant experience of working with GIS software and relational databases
  • Proven experience of undertaking spatial analyses with large dataset
  • Proven understanding of the complex issues surrounding data sharing, interoperability, integration and management of large datasets
  • Experience working with Protected Areas/Biodiversity related data
  • Experience in working with marine Protected Areas data or issues
  • Experience of presenting and promoting work to a wide range of audiences

The following skills would be also desirable:

  • Experience working with internationally operating organisations and agencies
  • Experience in the development and maintenance of web-based mapping systems and data sharing portals
  • Understanding of at least one other UN language would be beneficial

Projects by Protected Areas

EU Wildlife Trade Regulations

UNEP-WCMC provides a wide range of services to the European Commission in support of the effective implementation of the EU Wildlife Trade Regulations, the legislation that provides for the enforcement of CITES within the European Union.

Biodiversity Indicators Partnership

The CBD-mandated Biodiversity Indicators Partnership (BIP) is a global initiative that has operated since 2007, promoting and coordinating development and delivery of biodiversity indicators in support of the CBD and related Conventions, national and regional governments and a range of other sectors.
